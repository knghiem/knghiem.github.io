Someone famous in Source Title

I am a computer science student class of 2019 at Connecticut College, New London, CT. I am a proud Vietnamese, aspiring programmer, writer, and human rights advocate. Last summer, I interned at Yale Information Technology Services as a Project Coordinator, New Haven, CT. In spring 2018, I will be spending a semester at Columbia University in New York City, NY.

When I took intro to computer science in the fall of my sophomore year, something just clicked. I loved the class. I sat in the front row of the lecture hall and raised my hand to answer every question. I left out no bonus problems and spent lunch breaks learning about code. Until now, my love for the discipline has only grown deeper.

My ongoing development project for the last year is cameltours.org, a web application for creating multimedia presentations of cultural heritages, which aims to help communities tell authentic stories of their local heritage and enhance the onsite experience of visitors. I presented it at the IEEE MIT undergraduate research in technology in November 2017. In the future, I want to work more in the area of artificial intelligence and multimedia technology.

Although I now spend most of my time working in computer science and technology, I want to dedicate a significant portion of my blog to write about social issues. I spent my first year in college training as a social scientist, reading and writing extensively on power, class, race, and gender. Beside technical problems, issues of violence, discrimination, and injustice are constantly on my mind. My biggest social research projects are on the identity framing in Alt-Right movement and the connection between masculinity and homophobia.

Another portion of my website is reserved for my Vietnamese writing project since 2015, in which I reflect on my journey in the United States through a comical lense.

The most important person is my life is my mother, who inspired me from day one to persist through tough times, honor humility, grace, and kindness. My favorite place to visit is the beach. I always had dogs and puppies around the house before college and will adopt at least one on the day of graduation.
