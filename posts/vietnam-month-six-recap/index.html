Vietnam Month Six and 2022 Recap, question mark

Jan 5, 2023   ⋅   tags: writing

It’s good to know when to give up. I guess this is my announcement that I’m giving up on this monthly reflection project. I was still stuck on writing my October post. It was much easier in the first three months because most of my activities and thoughts back then were purely about transitioning back to Vietnam. But as I got more settled in, what I do and what I think about became complicated with present mundanity, past anxieties, and future worries. Since life has been pulling me in so many directions, I am no longer able to produce a substantial essay (even just 2500 words) about any topic, since they will be passing thoughts for a couple of days.

Regardless, I want to share with you that for the most part, I am doing well. I’ve been able to reconnect with a lot of old friends and meet new friends. I’ve been able to travel both domestically and internationally. And most importantly, I’ve been eating a lot of good food (already gaining more than 5 lbs!) Things are not perfect, of course. I drink a bit too much alcohol and take on more stressful work than before. I get taken advantage of sometimes because of my naivete. I turn in half-finished work and fall behind on chores. But I’m thankful that now at 25, I am able to give myself grace and march on forward.

It is unbelievable that I already spent six months(!!!) in Vietnam. Time is zooming by and 2023 is already here. This is going to be a major year for my professional, personal, and travel plans. Of course, I am nervous but I am diving in, head first.

I’d like to thank Lan and Ren for sticking with me throughout my journey and proofreading my poorly-written drafts. I’m figuring out new ways to share my thoughts with the world, I’ll see you then :)
