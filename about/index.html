About


Illustration collab with Hoang Tho

Khanh Nghiem /k⋅ahn ng⋅eem/ (b. 1997) grew up in Hanoi, Vietnam. He moved to the US in 2015 to attend Connecticut College. He graduated in 2019 with a BA in Computer Science and minors in and Sociology and Mathematics. He currently works at Google Cloud in Austin, TX as a Technical Account Manager.

Outside of his day job, Khanh pursues his various interests in technology, society, and the arts.

In the realm of technology, he mostly focuses on web technology. Since 2017, Khanh has been working on CamelTours, a free platform for users to create and publish self-guided tours. There have been 15+ tours created with CamelTours, mostly by heritage preservation societies, museum curators, and artists. He is also interested in UI/UX design, creative programming, data structures and algorithms.

In the realm of society, Khanh gravitates toward the topics of gender, nationalism, globalization, and their interconnectedness. He identifies as a feminist and believes in expanding institutional protection of girls and women worldwide, while promoting gender queer and non-binary identities. As a foreign student and expat, he’s curious to understand how individuals negotitate their national identities and how global forces shape local living conditions.

In the realm of the arts, Khanh dabbles in music, visual arts, and writing. He takes weekly singing lessons with his coach Grace Catherine where he practices pop and classical repertoires. He is self-teaching piano and hope to accompany his own singing some day. Khanh enjoys taking on projects in fashion photography, digital art, and data visualization. Khanh has an unfulfilled goal of publishing a complete body of narrative work.

Khanh welcomes discussions with experts and enthusiasts from any and all of these fields. Get in touch by sending a message to his linkedin, instagram, or email
