About

My name is Khanh Nghiem (b. 1997). I was born in Hanoi, Vietnam and moved to the US for college in 2015. I am currently living in Austin, TX. My day job is at Google Cloud, in a one-year talent development program where I received intensive training on cloud technology and business skills before rotating through different positions across the company. When the program ends in July, I get placed in one of the positions I have rotated through.

Outside of my day job, I study and work on projects in the fields of technology, social science, and art.

My technical projects currently focus on web-based applications. For the past 3 years, I have been working on CamelTours, a free platform for users to create and publish self-guided tours. There have been 15+ tours created with CamelTours, mostly by heritage preservation societies, museum curators, and artists. I am in the process of rebuilding a new version of CamelTours with a more modern technology stack and improved functionalities, set for release in Octobor 2020. Beside web, I am also interested in algorithms analysis and design, computer science education, and creative programming.

My social science projects mostly address the topics of gender, nationalism, and globalization. For the first two years of college, I was a sociology major. From this experience, I understood how sociology as a academic discipline can feel alienating to the communities it studies. My goal is to make insights from the field of sociology more accessible to the general audience through storytelling and stripping away academic jargons.

My artistic projects are in the forms of narrative nonfiction, design, and vocal music.
